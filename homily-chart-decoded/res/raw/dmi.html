DMI (Directional Movement Indicator)

a. Indicator Introduction

DMI (Directional Movement Indicator) was first proposed by J. Welles Wilder in 1978 in the book of New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems. DMI directs investors to avoid trading in consolidation market, once the market turns better, DMI immediately guides investors to enter the market and leave at the right moment. DMI is one of the indicators which have received much attention in recent years.

The basic principle of DMI is to seek The Point of Equilibrium between the buyers and sellers in the price ascending and descending process. That is the endless cycle of the change of supply-demand relation from intense to harmony, then intense, and then harmony through the changes of price.

b. Usage Principle

In using aspect, DMI is a tendency judgment system; therefore, it is limited by that whether market quotation tendency is obvious or not. If the market quotation maintains a very obvious trend, DMI will provide accurate trading signals. But in a consolidation market, the effect of DMI won't be so ideal. In tendency indicator system, we mainly analyze the relationship among rising line +DI, falling line –DI and ADX.

a)The function of rising line +DI and falling line –DI

When +DI upwards breaks through –DI, it shows that the new long come into the market and they would like to buy with higher price (since the price hits a new high, it makes +DI rise and –DI fall). It is a buy signal.

When –DI upwards breaks through +DI from lower, it shows that the new short come into the market and they would like to sell with lower price. It is a sell signal.

b) The functions of ADX

It is used to judge whether the market keeps the original main trend or not. When the trend obviously moves towards a single direction, ADX will increase no matter in an uptrend or downtrend. In other words, if ADX is higher than that of last trading day, the market will keep its original trend.

It is used to judge whether the market is in consolidation or not. When the market is in consolidation, ADX will decrease. Because the price has new highs as well as new lows, making the ascending line and descending line move closer and closer, so ADX decreases as well. When ADX falls below 20 and in a horizontal direction, we can judge that the market is in consolidation.

To judge whether the market is at the top or the bottom. When ADX turns into a descending trend from ascending tendency, it means the market will reverse. That is, the ascending trend will be over when ADX turns to fall in high position. Conversely, the descending trend will be over when ADX turns to fall in high position.
