KDJ (Stochastic Oscillator)

a. Indicator Introduction

KDJ (Stochastic Oscillator) which was created by George Lane is suitable for middle-short term technical analysis. The random KD line ideas are far more practical than moving average. Moving average is calculated only by closing price, so it can not show the true volatility of a period of market quotation. In other words, the intraday and the last few days' high price and low price cannot be reflected in moving average. So some experts gradually develop several more advanced technology theories which give full play to moving average. KD line is one of the representative masterpieces.

KDJ adopts %K and %D two lines in its chart. From the term of its design, KDJ is a comprehensive concept containing the advantages of momentum idea and relative strength indicator and moving average. From the term of its calculation, KDJ reflects the oversold and overbought phenomenon of the trend relative strength between the high or low price and the closing price. The major theoretical basis is: In the ascending trend, closing price closes to area of high price while in the descending trend, closing price closes to area of low price. In the stock and futures market, if the ascending trend doesn't turn downwards, price will tend to close at a high level. Similarly, if the descending trend doesn't turn upwards, closing price will tend to close at a low level. During the design of KDJ, the investors take full account of the estimates of the random volatility of short-term fluctuations. From the term of short-term market estimate, KDJ is more accurate and effective than moving average. In the term of predicting the short-term overselling and overbuying, KDJ is more sensitive than RSI; therefore, KDJ is widely used.

b. Usage Principle

a) The market is overbought, when %D is more than 80. The market is oversold, when %D is less than 20.

b) When KDJ deviates from price, it is generally a reversal signal.

c) When the value of %K is larger than that of %D, the current trend is ascending. When the value of %K is less than that of %D, the current trend is descending.

d) When KDJ forms golden cross that %K line upwards breaks through %D line, it is a buy signal.

e) When KDJ forms dead cross that %K line downwards breaks through %D line, it is a sell signal.

f) When KDJ hovers near 50, the reference value is little.

g) When the rising or falling speed of the %K & %D value weaken and the gradient trends to be gentle, it is a warning signal of reversion in short-term.

h) KD is not suitable for the stocks with small amount of issue and small trading amount, but it is quite accurate for Index and hot large-cap stocks.
