ROC

a. Indicator Introduction

ROC is ratio of intraday price to the price N days before. ROC, a method to measure price momentum, is introduced in a book Stock Market Trading System written by Gerald Apple and Fred Hitcher. If ROC is above 0-axis and continues to rise, it means the rising momentum increases. If ROC is above 0-axis but begins to fall, it means the rising momentum decreases and it is time to sell. If ROC is below 0-axis and continues to fall, it means the falling momentum increases. While ROC upwards reverses, it means the falling momentum is weakened and the buy signal appears.

b. Usage Principle

a) When ROC downwards breaks through 0-axis, it's time to reduce a position. When ROC upwards breaksthrough 0-axis, it's an opportunity to open a position.

b) When the stock price hits a new high, while ROC doesn't, it means rising force is weakened. We had better reduce a position owing to this top deviation.

c) When the stock price hits a new low, while ROC doesn't, it means falling force is weakened. We had better increase a position owing to this bottom deviation.

d) If ROC and the price ascend synchronously at a low level, it means the short-term trend is normal or there will be a short-term rebound. If ROC and the price descend synchronously at a high level, it means the short-term trend is normal or there will be a short-term correction.

e) ROC has a function of discovering overbought and oversold phenomenon. We can define different overbought and oversold areas according to different stocks.
