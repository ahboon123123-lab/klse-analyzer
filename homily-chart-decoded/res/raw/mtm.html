MTM

a. Indicator Introduction

MTM is a short-term indicator to measure the velocity of volatility. It focuses on analyzing the velocity of price volatility and the effects in process of price fluctuations' various acceleration, deceleration, inertia and also the mutual transfer between static and dynamic state. It can be used to forecast the relative strength of the long and the short to confirm the top or bottom reversal information.

b. Usage Principle

a) When MTM upwards breaks through the base date line, it is a signal to open a position. When MTM downwards breaks through the base date line, it is a sell signal.

b) When MTM upwards breaks through AMTM (10-day moving average), it is a signal to open a position. When MTM downwards breaks through AMTM (10-day moving average), it is a sell signal.

c) The stock price hits a new high in ascending trend while MTM doesn't meet up and turns up a top deviation, which means the rising power is weakened and we should be cautious of price downward reversal.

d) The stock price hits a new low in descending trend while MTM doesn't meet up and turns up a bottom deviation, which means the falling power is weakened, and we can catch the opportunity to take a position.

e) If stock price and MTM synchronously rise in the low area, there will be a rebound in short term. If the stock price and MTM synchronously fall in the high area, there will be a pullback in short term.

f) MTM also can be calculated based on Oscillation Point. Take 10-day MTM for example, the value of 10-day MTM equals to that intraday closing price divided by the close price 10-day before and multiplied by 100.
