DMA (Difference of Moving Average)

a. Indicator Introduction

DMA (Difference of Moving Average) judges the quotation by the difference of two averages of different cycles. That is the difference value between10-day average value and 50-day average value, and AMA is the average value of DMA.

b. Usage Principle

a) When DMA upwards breaks through AMA, and the market is ascending, it is a signal to buy; when AMA downwards breaks through DMA, and the market is descending, it is a signal to sell.

b) We can also use DMA to observe the price deviation.
