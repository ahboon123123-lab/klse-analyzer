MA

a. Indicator Introduction

A simple moving average is formed by computing the average (mean) price of a security over a specified number of periods. While it is possible to calculate moving averages from the Open, the High, and the Low, most moving averages are created using the Close.

Moving Average Group is formed by several Moving Averages. The analyst use the Moving Average Group to analyze the price trend.

b. Characteristic

The main characteristics of the moving average are as the following:

a) Trend Direction

Moving Average presents the trend of price movement so it has the nature of trend.

b) Stability

The most particular characteristic of Moving Average is that the price appears correction while the Moving Average is upward. Not until the downtrend is obvious, the Moving Average is downward. The longer the term of the moving average is, the stabler it is. On the contrary,the stability is weak.

c) Ascending/Descending Acceleration

Moving average advances as it is upwards/downwards penetrated by price. It is the bullish/bearish formation which produces the support/resistance power. Short-term moving average is faster than mid-term or long-term one. Although there are some differences between the moving averages of different periods, they have the power to accelerate the price movement.
