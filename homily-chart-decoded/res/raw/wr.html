WR (Williams Overbought/Oversold Index)

a. Indicator Introduction

WR (Williams Overbought/Oversold Index) is proposed for the first time in the book of How I made a million index written by Larry Williams in 1973. WR is an indicator which is used to measure the quotation fluctuation based on the principle of opening a position when the market turns strong while closing a position when the market turns weak. WR is a technical indicator for short-term analysis and it is a valuable transaction reference for the investors.

b. Usage Principle

a) When WR is above 85, the market is oversold and the quotation will come to the bottom.

b) When WR is below 15, the market is overbought and the quotation will soon come to the top.

c) If WR is combined with RSI, the two may complement each other to discover the oversold and overbought phenomenon. This combination can make a more accurate judgment to the future market.

d) If WR is combined with momentum oscillators in the same market cycle, we can confirm the top and bottom of the price.

e) When we use WR as a measure tool, it is not easy to miss the main ascending market and to be hung up in the high area. However, WR is too sensitive; we had better combine it with other less sensitive indicators such as relative strength index in operation.
