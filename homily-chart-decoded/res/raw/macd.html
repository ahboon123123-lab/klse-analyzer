MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

a. Indicator Introduction

MACD which is created by Gerald is a technical analysis tool commonly used in USA. MACD absorbs the merits of moving average; when the trend is obvious, using moving average to judge the trading opportunity is very effective, but when in consolidation, its signals are frequent and inaccurate. MACD based on moving average principle can overcome the defect of frequent false signals and utilize the largest merits of moving average.

b. Usage Principle

a) When DIF and DEA are both positive(above the zero axis), the market is bullish. It's a signal to open a position when DIF upwards breaks through DEA, while it's only a signal to close a position when DIF downwards breaks through DEA.

b) When DIF and DEA are both negative (below the zero axis), the market is bearish. It is a sell signal when DIF downwards breaks through DEA, while it's only a rally signal when DIF upwards breaks through DEA.

c) When the DEA deviates from the candlestick trend, it is a reversal signal.

d) When in consolidation, there may be a high rate of mistakes when we use DEA. However, we can appropriately make up for the deficiencies through the combination with RSI & KD.

e) Based on the analysis to MACD bar diagram, when the bars turn into negative, it is a sell signal; when the bars turn into positive, it is a buy signal.
