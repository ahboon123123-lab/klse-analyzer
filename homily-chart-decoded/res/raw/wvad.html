WVAD (Williams' Variable Accumulation/Distribution)

a. Indicator Introduction

WVAD (Williams' Variable Accumulation/Distribution) is a volume-weighted indicator created by Larry Williams. It is a volume weighted indicator. The area between closing price and opening price is the reference area, and the resistance area is over the reference area while below it is the support area. The ratio of reference area to the total intraday fluctuation is used to measure the share of the intraday turnover in the reference area.

b. Usage Principle

a) When WVAD is positive, it means the long win the advantage over the short, and it's an opportunity to buy in.

b) When WVAD is negative, it means the short win the advantage over the long, and it's an opportunity to sell.

c) When WVAD changes to positive from negative, it is a buy signal for long-term trading.

d) When WVAD changes to negative from positive, it is a sell signal for long-term trading.
