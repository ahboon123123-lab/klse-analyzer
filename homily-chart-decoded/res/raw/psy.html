PSY

a. Indicator Introduction

PSY focuses on the buy or sell psychology of the investors in a certain period. PSY is generally drawn adopting 10-day PSI as sample. For example, if there are 5 days rising and 5 days falling in 10 trading days, the PSI is 50% (5/10). Then mark the percentage in the chart and connect the percentage of every trading day and the formed line is called PSI. PSI should be com-bined with candlestick chart to find the overbought or oversold situation. From the view of PSI, the low point of oversold phenomenon will appear twice at the bottom, if we find it has a heavy overbought phenomenon in one day and the price is impossibly lower than this point, it is an opportunity to open a position when PSI falls again to the first low point, vice versa. There are more than two trading points before an ascending or a descending quotation, so we have sufficient time to make a final decision.

b. Usage Principle

a) Before an ascending trend, the oversold low points usually emerge twice; while before a descending trend, the overbought high points usually emerge twice.

b) 25% ～ 75% is the normal state for analysis.

c) When PSY is over 75% or below 25%, it is an overbought or oversold signal, the chance of price falling or rising enlarging, and we can close or open a position.

d) When denseness of high points appears twice, it is a sell signal; when denseness of low points appears twice, it is a buy signal.
