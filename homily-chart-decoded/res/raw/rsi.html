RSI

a. Indicator Introduction

RSI (Relative Strength Index) is one of the trading methods proposed in the book New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems written by Welles Wilder JR, an American author.

The theory of RSI points out that, in a normal market, the price can keep stabilization only when the strength of the long and the short keep balance. RSI is the ratio of the average of total rise amplitude to the average of total fluctuation amplitude in a certain period.

b. Usage Principle

a) When the value of 9-day RSI is above 80, the market is in overbought status; when it is below 20, the market is in oversold status; when it is between 20 and 80, the market is normal.

b) When RSI is below 50, the market is weak; when it is above 50, the market is strong.

c) When M shape appears near 80, it is time to reduce a position; when W bottom appears near 10, it is time to increase a position.

d) When the market is in consolidation and RSI rises higher and higher, it means the long is relatively strong and the future market is expected to rise; when RSI falls lower and lower, it is a signal to reduce a position.

e) If the price is still in consolidation while RSI has completed the consolidation, the price will subsequently break away from consolidation area.

f) When RSI deviates from the price, it is generally a reversal signal. It stands for the trend reversal, so we should be very careful to choose the right trading time.

g) We can determine the trading time combining the fast RSI with slow RSI. When 9-day RSI upwards breaks through 14-day RSI, it is a signal to open a position. When 9-day RSI downwards breaks through 14-day RSI, it is a signal to close a position. Especially, it is relatively reliable when the buy signal emerges below 30-axis and the sell signal emerges above 70-axis.

h) When the resistance line (downtrend line) in RSI chart presents an angle from negative 15 degree to negative 30 degree, the resistance is the most significant. If the angle of resistance line is too steep, the resistance line will soon be broken and lose its resistancemeaning. On the contrary, when the support line (uptrend line) in RSI chart presents an angle from positive 15degree to positive 30 degree, the support is the most significant. If the angle of support line is too steep, it will soon be broken and lose its support meaning.
