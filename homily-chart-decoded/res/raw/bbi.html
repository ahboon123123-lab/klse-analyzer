BBI

a. Indicator Introduction

BBI integrates moving averages of different days and is calculated from four arithmetic average prices of 3- & 6- & 12- & 24-day.

b. Usage Principle

a) Since BBI value contains parts of weights of different days' moving averages, thus it represents the different interests of each moving average.

b) BBI is a special product of the moving average principles. When we apply the trading signals of BBI, we can basically refer to the principles of moving average.

c) When close price downwards breaks through BBI in high area, it's a sell signal.

d) When close price upwards breaks through BBI in low area, it's a buy signal.

e) When BBI rises from low position and the price stands above BBI, it means the bull takes the mastering position and we can hold position.

f) When BBI falls from high position and the price stands bellow BBI, it means the bear takes the mastering position and it is not suitable to take a position.
